# Baseline_Supplies — Packaging Design Brief

A self-contained brief for designing retail packaging that matches the existing
brand and website. Give this entire document to the designer or design AI.

---

## 1. Who we are

**Baseline_Supplies** is a sports-nutrition company that sells exactly one
product: raw micronized **creatine monohydrate** powder. No blends, no flavors,
no proprietary anything. The entire brand is built on **radical price and
ingredient transparency** — we publish the cost per gram and the full
(one-line) ingredient list everywhere, including on the front of the site.

Brand personality: dry, confident, a little deadpan. The website's front door
is a photo of shrink-wrapped powder bricks stamped "CREATINE" styled like a
contraband seizure — we're in on the joke that our product looks like a
controlled substance because it's *that pure and that raw*. The humor is
committed but never cartoonish; execution stays premium and disciplined.

Tagline register (examples from the live site — match this voice):
- "One ingredient. One price. No blend to hide behind."
- "Raw monohydrate · 500g · $0.056 / gram · nothing else"
- "That's the whole label."
- "Priced like an ingredient, not a brand."

---

## 2. The product being packaged

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Product | Micronized creatine monohydrate, raw unflavored powder |
| Format | 500g per bag |
| Servings | 100 × 5g scoops |
| Ingredient list | Creatine monohydrate. Nothing else. |
| Price story | $28.00 single / $23.80 subscription / $23.00 bulk — cost-per-gram ($0.056/g) is printed proudly, not hidden |

Packaging format: primarily a **stand-up resealable pouch** (500g powder).
A secondary concept for a shipping/bulk carton is welcome — the brand joke
supports "brick" formats (see §5).

---

## 3. Visual identity (use these exact values)

### Color palette
| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | `#171717` | Primary background or primary text — dominant neutral |
| Paper | `#faf6ef` | Warm off-white — the other half of every composition |
| Signal Orange | `#f97316` | THE accent. Used sparingly: one underscore, one rule line, one price. Never flooded |
| Graphite | `#5b5750` | Secondary/caption text on light backgrounds |
| Void | `#0a0a09` | Near-black, used for dramatic dark compositions |

Rules: high-contrast two-tone (ink-on-paper or paper-on-ink) with orange as a
single disciplined accent. **No gradients. No purple. No neon palettes. No
metallic foils implied by color.**

### Typography
| Role | Typeface | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Display / wordmark support | **Fraunces** (Black 900, tight) | Big editorial serif — used for the brand statement and headlines |
| Data / specs / prices | **JetBrains Mono** (Regular–Medium, uppercase, letterspaced) | Everything numeric or factual is set in monospace — this is the transparency signature |
| Body | **Instrument Sans** | Quiet supporting copy |

### Logo
The brand has an athletic "BS" monogram with an orange slash, plus a
"Baseline_Supplies" wordmark in a dark italic industrial sans. The signature
brand mark everywhere else is the **orange underscore**: `Baseline_Supplies`,
`CREATINE_`. The underscore is always Signal Orange when the surrounding text
is not.

### Signature graphic device
The website's hero element is a **regulatory Supplement Facts panel** treated
as brand art — thick ink rules, mono type, and the punchline rows:
"Other Ingredients: **None**" / "That's the whole label." Carry this onto the
packaging: the facts panel is not fine print here, it's the front-of-pack
hero graphic or a major design element.

---

## 4. What the packaging must communicate (in priority order)

1. **CREATINE** — the word, huge. The product is the brand.
2. **Transparency as design**: cost per gram and the one-line ingredient list
   displayed like a spec sheet, front of pack. Numbers set in JetBrains Mono.
3. **500g · 100 servings · 5g scoop** — utilitarian dosing info, clearly.
4. Baseline_Supplies wordmark/monogram — present but smaller than the product
   name. We sell the ingredient, not the logo.

Mandatory copy elements to design with:
- "CREATINE_" or "CREATINE MONOHYDRATE" (dominant)
- "Baseline_Supplies" (secondary)
- "500g · 100 servings · $0.056/g" (or equivalent spec line)
- Supplement-facts panel including "Other Ingredients: None"
- Optional back-of-pack line: "That's the whole label."

---

## 5. Creative territory (the "brick" bit)

The brand leans into the visual comedy of pure white powder in utilitarian
packaging: shrink-wrap texture, kraft-paper-and-stencil "evidence bag" or
"cargo brick" energy, stamped/stenciled type, batch numbers, tape seals.
Think: *what if a premium specialty brand packaged creatine the way
contraband bricks look in a police photo* — but executed with the restraint
of a high-end coffee roaster.

Encouraged motifs: stencil stamps ("100% MONOHYDRATE", batch/lot codes,
net-weight stamps), string-and-button closures, plain-wrap aesthetics,
customs/freight typography, the orange underscore as a tamper-seal strip.

Keep it classy: no drug references in words, no cannabis-leaf-adjacent
imagery, no glow effects. The joke lives in the *format*, never the copy.

---

## 6. Hard bans

- No purple/violet/indigo anywhere
- No gradients (especially purple-blue or orange fades)
- No glassmorphism, glow, or soft 3D blob shapes
- No emoji, no starbursts ("NEW!"), no badge clutter
- No generic supplement tropes: flexing silhouettes, lightning bolts around
  muscles, carbon-fiber texture, "X-treme" energy
- No fake claims ("39% more gains") — every number printed must be real
  (weight, servings, price per gram)

---

## 7. Deliverables to ask the design AI for

1. Front-of-pack design for a 500g stand-up pouch (both colorways: ink-on-paper
   and paper-on-ink)
2. Back of pack featuring the supplement-facts panel as hero
3. One "brick"/bulk-carton concept exploring the §5 territory
4. Flat-lay product photography direction (props: raw powder, scoop, kraft
   paper, shrink wrap — shot on `#faf6ef` or `#0a0a09`)

Judge every concept against: *"Does this look like a designed object from a
brand obsessed with transparency — or like a supplement?"* If it looks like a
supplement, start over.
